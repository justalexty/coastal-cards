# Croneslist Design Notes

## The Psychology of Broom Scarcity

The Croneslist broom system creates urgency through **time-based scarcity** rather than quality variance:

### Why This Works:
1. **FOMO (Fear of Missing Out)**: Every missed broom hurts when you're walking everywhere
2. **Irregular Rewards**: Random posting times = compulsive checking behavior  
3. **Near Misses**: "Sold 2 minutes ago" is more frustrating than never seeing it
4. **Cash Pressure**: Having money ready means tough choices (skip meals?)
5. **Social Proof**: Seller names like "RentDueTomorrow" show others struggling too

### Player Behavior:
- Check Croneslist first thing in morning
- Check during every "downtime" moment
- Feel genuine excitement when notification arrives
- Experience real disappointment when too slow
- Celebrate getting ANY broom, no matter how ugly

### The Ugly Broom Joy:
When you finally snag one:
- "Missing half the bristles (still flies)" becomes a badge of honor
- "Duct tape holds the handle" is YOUR duct-taped handle
- The aesthetic flaws become character, not defects
- First flight after walking for weeks = pure euphoria

### Notification Strategy:
- Compact mirror buzzes/glows when broom posts
- Different intensity based on price (cheaper = more urgent)
- "While you were reading cards, 3 brooms sold" guilt
- Missed broom notifications create regret/urgency

### Economic Balance:
- Most brooms $200-250 (8-10 days of perfect earnings)
- Occasional "steal" at $180 (still 7+ days of work)
- Rare $280 "expensive" ones last longer (20+ minutes)
- Forces choice: save for broom or eat well?

### Emotional Arc:
1. **Days 1-7**: "I'll get one eventually"
2. **Days 8-14**: Checking constantly, growing desperate
3. **Days 15-20**: Every missed listing hurts
4. **Day 21+**: Would buy ANYTHING that flies
5. **Purchase Day**: Best feeling in the game
6. **First Flight**: Remember why you're a witch

This system makes a simple purchase into a memorable victory through artificial scarcity and time pressure.
